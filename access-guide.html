What is FreelanceHub?
An overview of what the app does and who it's for

FreelanceHub is your all-in-one operating system for freelance work. It was built for designers, developers, video editors, photographers, copywriters, consultants, coaches — anyone running a one-person business who is tired of juggling a CRM, an invoice tool, a spreadsheet, a time tracker, and a notes app all at once.

No account. No subscription. No installation. Open the link in your browser and you're inside. Your data is stored locally on your device — not on any server, not accessible to anyone but you. Close the tab, come back tomorrow: everything is still there.

💡 How data storage works

FreelanceHub uses your browser's built-in localStorage — a private storage space on your own device. Think of it as a notebook that lives inside your browser. Your data persists even when you close the tab, as long as you don't clear your browser's site data. Export a JSON backup from Settings to keep a safe copy.

FreelanceHub Clients & Projects
Client Manager
Keep all your client information in one place

The Clients section is your contact book. For each client you can store their name, email, phone number, company, status, and personal notes.

Add a client — click "Add Client" at the top right and fill in their details. Only the name is required.
Client status — mark each client as Active, Inactive, or Prospect to filter your list quickly.
Notes — use the notes field for anything useful: billing preferences, time zones, communication style, contract details.
Search & filter — use the search bar or the status filter (All / Active / Inactive / Prospect) to find clients instantly.
Project Pipeline
A visual Kanban board from first contact to final payment

Projects are organized in a Kanban board with four columns that represent the lifecycle of a freelance project:

To Do

Projects that are planned but not yet started. Use this for confirmed work you haven't begun.

In Progress

Active projects you are currently working on. Move cards here as soon as you start.

Delivered

Work that has been submitted to the client but not yet paid. Awaiting invoice settlement.

Paid

Completed and paid projects. Your archive of finished work.

Move a project — click the arrow button on a project card to advance it to the next stage.
Link to a client — assign each project to one of your saved clients for easy tracking.
Rate & due date — optionally add a budget and deadline so you never miss an important date.

FreelanceHub Finances & Dashboard
Finance Tracker
Log income and expenses, track your real balance

The Finances section lets you record every income and expense related to your freelance activity. At the top you always see three live numbers: total income, total expenses, and your current balance.

Add income — click "Add Income" and enter the amount, a description, date, and optionally the project it belongs to. Example: "Client invoice — Website redesign — $2,400".
Add expense — record business costs like software subscriptions, equipment, or subcontractors. Example: "Adobe CC subscription — $55".
Monthly chart — a bar chart shows your income vs. expenses over the last 6 months so you can spot trends at a glance.
Filter & search — filter by type (income / expense) or search by description to find any transaction quickly.
💡 Tip — Link transactions to projects

When adding income, link it to the corresponding project. This way you can track exactly which projects have been paid and which are still pending, directly from the project board.

Dashboard
Your business at a glance — updated in real time

The Dashboard is your home screen. Every time you open the app, it shows you the four numbers that matter most right now:

Income this month

Total income recorded in the current calendar month.

Active clients

Number of clients currently marked as "Active" in your client list.

Active projects

Projects in "To Do" or "In Progress" status — work currently in motion.

Paid this month

Income transactions dated in the current month that are marked as paid.

Click any stat card to navigate directly to that section. The income chart at the bottom shows your last 6 months of activity.

FreelanceHub Saving Your Data & Settings
Saving & backing up your data
How to keep your data safe and transfer it between devices

FreelanceHub automatically saves every change you make to your browser's localStorage — you never need to click "Save" for your data. However, localStorage is tied to a specific browser on a specific device. To keep your data safe long-term, use the export options in Settings.

↓
Export all data (JSON) — recommended backup Settings → "Export all data (JSON)". This downloads a single file containing all your clients, projects, and transactions. Keep this file somewhere safe (Google Drive, Dropbox, email to yourself). Use it to restore your data on any device.
↑
Import data (JSON) — restore or transfer Settings → "Import data (JSON)". Select your backup file and all your data will be restored instantly. Use this to switch browsers, switch devices, or recover from an accidental reset.
xls
Export to Excel (.xlsx) Settings → "Export to Excel". Generates a spreadsheet with three sheets: Clients, Projects, and Transactions. Useful for sharing with an accountant or creating reports.
csv
Export individual CSVs Export clients, projects, or transactions separately as CSV files — compatible with Excel, Google Sheets, and most accounting tools.
⚠️ Important — protect your data

If you clear your browser history, cookies, or site data, localStorage will be erased. Export a JSON backup regularly — at least once a week — so you never lose your records. Store the backup file in a cloud folder for extra safety.
